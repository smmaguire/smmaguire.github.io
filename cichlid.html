Cichlid networks

Below is a visualization of social networks of groups of Neolamprologus multifasciatus cichlid fish. They're the worlds smallest kind of cichlid! The males build and guard nests made out of snail shells. Females will lay their eggs inside the shells. We wanted to know what happens when we make one male's nest more attractive by adding shells? It turns out that they get more aggression from rival males as well as more attention from neighboring females. In addition to that there is another much larger species of cichlid that uses these shells! They like to lay their eggs inside the shells, however they are much too large to clean out the shells on their own. They steal the territories from the poor Neolamprologus multi males. It turns out that adding shells to the male's territory also increases their risk of having their territory taken over by the other species. Use the drop down menu to see how each group's social network changes each day. You may notice a large shift on day 4, this is the first day of the adding shells manipulation. In some groups the social connections drop off after day 5 or 6. These groups were taken over by the other species and chased off! Check out the paper: http://goo.gl/8fdmmr
